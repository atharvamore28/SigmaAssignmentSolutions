Q2. What are HTML Entities? List out 5 commonly used HTML entities.
Ans. 

   HTML entities are special codes used in HTML documents to represent characters that have a special meaning in HTML, such as reserved characters (<, >) or characters that cannot be easily typed using a keyboard.

   Five commonly used HTML entities are : 
   &lt;: Represents the less-than symbol ("<").
   &gt;: Represents the greater-than symbol (">").
   &amp;: Represents the ampersand symbol ("&").
   &quot;: Represents the double quotation mark (").
   &apos;: Represents the single quotation mark (apostrophe) (').
